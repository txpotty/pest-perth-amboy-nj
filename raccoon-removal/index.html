Identifying Common Issues Requiring Raccoon Removal in Perth Amboy, NJ

Recognizing the signs of a raccoon problem early can save you time, money, and stress. Several indicators point to the potential presence of raccoons on your property. Being aware of these signs allows you to take prompt action and contact professionals for assistance. It's better to address a potential issue sooner rather than later to avoid further complications.

Noises in the Attic or Walls

Hearing scratching, thumping, or chattering sounds coming from your attic or within your walls, especially at night, is a strong indicator of animal activity. Raccoons are nocturnal, meaning they are most active during the nighttime hours. These sounds can be particularly disturbing when you are trying to sleep. The sounds may intensify as they move around or build nests. These noises are a clear signal that unwanted guests have taken up residence.

These noises are often a direct result of raccoons nesting or foraging for food within your home's structure. They may be tearing at insulation, chewing on wood, or moving materials to create a comfortable den. Our raccoon removal service includes a thorough inspection to pinpoint the source of these noises and determine the extent of the infestation. We can then safely and effectively remove the animals and address any damage they have caused.

Overturned Trash Cans

One of the most common and obvious signs of raccoon presence is finding your trash cans overturned and their contents scattered. Raccoons are opportunistic eaters and are highly attracted to discarded food. They possess strong paws and can easily open or knock over unsecured garbage bins. This messy behavior is a clear indication that raccoons are regularly visiting your property in search of an easy meal.

Securing your trash cans with tight-fitting lids or storing them in a secure location like a garage or shed can help deter raccoons. However, if this problem persists despite your efforts, it suggests a more established presence that requires professional intervention. Our service includes advising you on effective methods for securing attractants and preventing raccoons from accessing your garbage.

Damage to Property

Raccoons are known to cause significant damage as they attempt to gain entry into homes and other structures or when they are nesting. This damage can include torn shingles, damaged vents, bent gutters, or holes in fascia boards. In attics, they may tear up insulation for nesting material or chew on electrical wires, posing a fire hazard. They can also cause damage to gardens, sheds, and other outdoor structures. This type of property damage is not just unsightly; it can compromise the integrity of your building.

Repairing raccoon-related damage is an important part of the overall removal process. Our team can identify the extent of the damage during our inspection and recommend necessary repairs to secure your property against future intrusions. Addressing the damage is crucial for both aesthetic reasons and to prevent other pests or further wildlife from gaining access. We can even assist with wildlife control in general.

Presence of Feces

Raccoon feces can be a serious health hazard as they can contain roundworm eggs, which can be harmful to humans and pets. Raccoons often establish communal latrines, areas where they repeatedly defecate. These latrines are typically located in elevated areas like decks, roofs, or attics, but can also be found at the base of trees or other prominent locations. The appearance of these concentrated areas of droppings is a strong indicator of an ongoing raccoon problem. The feces are typically tubular and dark in color, often containing undigested food remnants.

It is important to handle raccoon feces with extreme caution and wear protective gear. Our raccoon removal service includes safe and thorough cleanup of these contaminated areas, followed by disinfection to eliminate any potential health risks. Proper disposal of raccoon feces is crucial for preventing the spread of disease. We have the necessary equipment and knowledge to handle this safely and effectively, ensuring your property is clean and safe after the raccoons are gone. Don't try to clean this yourself; it's best left to professionals who understand the risks involved.

Seeing Raccoons During the Day

While raccoons are primarily nocturnal, seeing one during the daytime may indicate that it is sick or that their den has been disturbed. However, it could also mean the raccoon is lactating and making extra trips to find food for its young. Regardless of the reason, the presence of a raccoon during daylight hours is a cause for concern and warrants investigation. A raccoon acting strangely during the day, such as being lethargic or aggressive, could be sick and potentially carry rabies. If you observe a raccoon exhibiting unusual behavior during the day, it's crucial to maintain a safe distance and avoid direct contact. They may frequent areas near your property line, like fences or trees, so keep an eye out. If you also have issues with squirrels or rodents, we can help with that too.

If you see a raccoon during the day, especially one that appears to be acting unusually, it's best to contact a professional wildlife removal service immediately. They can assess the situation and determine the appropriate course of action. Never attempt to approach or handle a raccoon yourself, regardless of the time of day. These animals can be unpredictable, especially if they feel threatened or are sick. Calling for professional help is the safest way to handle a raccoon sighting during daylight hours and ensure the safety of yourself, your family, and your pets. Pet Food Disappearing

Leaving pet food outdoors, especially overnight, is a major attractant for raccoons and other wildlife. If you notice that your pet's food dish is consistently empty in the morning, it's highly likely that a raccoon or another animal has been raiding it. Raccoons have an excellent sense of smell and are easily drawn to readily available food sources. This can create a regular feeding pattern for the raccoons, encouraging them to frequent your property. It's important to take steps to eliminate this easily accessible food source to deter them. Consider bringing pet food indoors at night as a simple preventative measure.

To prevent raccoons from accessing pet food, store it in airtight containers indoors. Only feed your pets what they will consume in one sitting, and clean up any leftover food promptly. Footprints or Tracks

Raccoons leave behind distinctive footprints that can help identify their presence. Their tracks have five toes on both their front and back paws, and the front paws resemble small human hands. You may find these tracks in mud, dust, or even snow around your property, particularly near trash cans, entry points, or areas where they are foraging. Learning to identify these tracks can be a useful tool for homeowners. The size of the tracks can vary depending on the age of the raccoon.
